Screen Time vs Sleep Time: Who Wins?

A new study reveals the surprising link between smartphone use before bed and our sleep quality.

Written by Team Example

Introduction

In today’s world, as the time spent on digital devices increases, our sleeping time has seen a gradual decrease. Because of this, people suspect that future generations might get too addicted to these digital devices and be more chronically online. This ongoing trend has caused some concerns for future generations.

Main Passage

Screen time can affect sleep time by messing with our track of time because of the continuous dopamine boost if used improperly, but when used properly and effectively, we can maximize our productivity when using these digital devices. By utilizing resources provided by devices, we can do better activities that far outweighs the downsides of using these digital devices improperly.

One of our interviewees, a student well-known for sleeping in class, Fabiyan Johnata, said that too much screen time can reduce sleeping time and even cause fatigue when waking up. Knowing that, his screen time is still significantly higher than his sleeping time (more than double). Another student we interviewed, Aurelio Nathaniel, who said that “The real world is still beautiful and needs to be explored more”, also has more screen time than his own sleeping time. This shows the growing addiction to digital devices in this generation and the potential risks it brings to sleep time, as well as to physical and mental health.

However, the other student we interviewed, Muhammad Ghani, manages to balance his screen time without losing too much sleeping time, keeping a relatively healthy lifestyle. He says that “I usually spend more time in school studying and playing with friends in the real world”, this shows that when utilized efficiently and correctly, spending more time on digital devices can actually help increase productivity without diminishing sleep time. Such as studying or working at night because almost all other activities at night decrease so we can do those activities more calmly and comfortably.

Conclusion

In conclusion, screen time can have both positive and negative effects depending on how it is used. While excessive use of digital devices may reduce sleep time and affect physical and mental health, proper and balanced usage can also improve productivity and support daily activities. Therefore, managing screen time wisely is important so that technology can be beneficial without negatively affecting our health and sleep.
